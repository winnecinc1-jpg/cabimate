Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our cabinet and bath hardware collections.

Should I choose cabinet knobs or drawer pulls for my cabinets?
+
There is no "right" rule, but functionality is key. Drawer pulls offer better leverage, making them ideal for heavy drawers and lower cabinetry. Cabinet knobs are often preferred for upper cabinets where you want a lighter, more minimalist aesthetic. Many modern designs successfully mix both for a balanced look—using knobs on doors and pulls on drawers.
How do I determine the right size for my cabinet pulls?
+
A popular design guideline is to choose a cabinet pull that is approximately one-third the width of the drawer front. For a more contemporary or modern look, you can opt for longer pulls that span half or even the full width of the drawer. When in doubt, sizing up usually provides a more impactful, premium appearance
What finish works best for modern kitchen and cabinet hardware?
+
While trends evolve, finishes like Matte Black, Brushed Brass, and Amber Brass are currently favored for their ability to add contrast and warmth. The best approach is to coordinate your cabinet hardware with your existing fixed elements, such as faucets, light fixtures, or appliance finishes, to ensure a cohesive, professional design.
How do I measure for replacement cabinet hardware?
+
If you are replacing existing knobs and pulls, the most important measurement is the "center-to-center" distance—the space between the centers of your two screw holes. Most modern hardware is measured in millimeters (e.g., 96mm, 128mm, 160mm). Matching this distance ensures your new hardware will align perfectly without the need for drilling new holes.
